Organization Application

Why does your org want to participate in Google Summer of Code?

SimpleCPU is aimed towards students and researchers helping them learn and easily carry out CPU simulations in an intuitive way. This project is an endeavour to motivate students towards Computer Architecture and HDL coding. We intend to make SimpleCPU a global project being accepted and used at universities world-wide. We would need help to spread SimpleCPU project globally. Our goal is to enable system-designers and researchers to rapidly evaluate new ideas in the field of processor design, cache design and other aspects involved in computer architecture research. We are trying to establish this project as part of the labs for Computer architecture courses at various universities. This would give students a great insight on the entire system-on-chip design and verification flow. Our aim is to add a complete processor design system such that universities can adopt SimpeCPU by next fall and believe GSoC would give us world wide exposure.

How will you keep mentors engaged with their students?

Most of the mentors of the organisation have been a part of SimpleCPU since the very beginning and are very excited to see it grow. As a result all the mentors would be in general be interested in engaging with their student. They would be happy to help not only their assigned student but anyone who wishes to contribute to SimpleCPU. We plan to make the best use of the community bonding period, giving enough time to every mentor/student to interact with each other. This would definitely enable a healthy relationship between the mentors and the students. As part of this period we would also love to engage in discussions related to upcoming technologies in this field and to know more about the student's interest and background. Also, mentors are encouraged to conduct a weekly review with their student to keep the community updated on the progress.

How will you help your students stay on schedule to complete their projects?

All the projects mentioned in the Ideas page have a well-planned timeline which would help students and mentors to keep a check on the schedule. We would also be providing all the necessary details and documents about the project and the expected outcome so that the student is well aware about the project. We would want students to come up with a timeline depending upon on their academic schedule (exams/project submissions/career fairs). We would try to have daily discussions with the students and make sure that the student is able to make progress on the project as per the plan and doesn't get stuck in between. We would encourage the selected students to maintain a public blog showcasing their work/activities over the period of the program. Along with this we would also like to add student's work to be a part of the official SimpleCPU repository. This would definitely be encouraging for the student to see their work being used by others and motivate them to stay on schedule.

How will you get your students involved in your community during GSoC?

We would be thrilled to be a part of GSoC program and would be equally excited to involve students in our community. As part of the program we would encourage student to interact with us as often as possible, before and during the application & evaluation period. To facilitate this we have a public IRC channel and a mailing list. The IRC channel would help students to familiarise themselves with the community and would get them acquainted with the developers. We believe that effective communication will be one of the major requirements for a successful application. We will treat students in the same way we treat all our new contributors at SimpleCPU. By encouraging students to communicate directly with the entire community instead of privately with their mentors, we will instil a spirit of open source development amongst the students.

How will you keep students involved with your community after GSoC?

The whole reason why a student would stick with us after GSoC would depend how we treated them during the course of the program. By treating students like any other SimpleCPU developer and by enabling them to be part of almost all the community decisions right from the start would make them more inclined towards our community. Since we have a lot of planned future work and new ideas to work upon, we would try our best that the students take up further work at SimpleCPU project. We would also like to keep a personal contact with the students before, during and after GSoC making them be an integral part of the community.
